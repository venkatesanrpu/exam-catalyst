Core Concepts

  1. Topic: Interactions Lesson: Coevolution Clarification: Reciprocal evolutionary change in interacting species driven by natural selection imposed by each on the other; requires heritable variation and consistent interaction. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced

Topic: Interactions Lesson: Co-adaptations Clarification: Matched traits in interacting species that increase fitness in the interaction context (e.g., flower–pollinator trait matching); distinguishing adaptation from mere correlation. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  3. Topic: Interactions Lesson: Evolutionary arms race Clarification: Escalating reciprocal adaptations and counter-adaptations (offense/defense) often producing rapid trait evolution and strong selection. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  4. Topic: Interactions Lesson: Red Queen hypothesis Clarification: Continuous evolution is required to maintain relative fitness in a biotic environment that is itself evolving; commonly applied to host–parasite and predator–prey dynamics. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  5. Topic: Interactions Lesson: Cospeciation Clarification: Parallel speciation of interacting lineages producing congruent phylogenies; typical in tight host–symbiont/parasite associations; recognizing alternative causes of congruence. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  6. Topic: Interactions Lesson: Predator–prey interactions (evolutionary outcomes) Clarification: Selection generated by predation risk and prey defenses shaping behavior, morphology, life history, and predator counter-strategies. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  7. Topic: Interactions Lesson: Crypsis (camouflage) Clarification: Traits that reduce detection by predators or prey via background matching, disruptive coloration, or masquerade; effectiveness depends on environment and predator perception. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  8. Topic: Interactions Lesson: Mimicry Clarification: Resemblance to another organism/object affecting predator behavior; major forms include Batesian, Müllerian, and aggressive mimicry. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced
