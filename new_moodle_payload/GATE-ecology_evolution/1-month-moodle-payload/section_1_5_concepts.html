Related Concepts

  1. Topic: Ecosystems Structure and Function Lesson: Energy flow vs nutrient cycling Clarification: Energy flows one-way through ecosystems (with losses as heat), while nutrients cycle among pools and are conserved in matter transformations.   2. Topic: Ecosystems Structure and Function Lesson: Ecological efficiency and trophic transfer efficiency Clarification: Fraction of energy/biomass transferred between trophic levels, often summarized conceptually as ∼10% but variable by system and trophic pathway.   3. Topic: Ecosystems Structure and Function Lesson: Detrital pathway and decomposition Clarification: Transfer of organic matter to detritus and decomposers; major route of energy and nutrient recycling in many ecosystems. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  4. Topic: Ecosystems Structure and Function Lesson: Stoichiometry and nutrient limitation Clarification: Elemental ratios (e.g., C:N:P) shaping nutrient demand, limitation, and consumer-resource interactions. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  5. Topic: Ecosystems Structure and Function Lesson: Mineralization and immobilization Clarification: Microbial conversion of organic nutrients to inorganic forms (mineralization) versus uptake and incorporation into biomass (immobilization).   6. Topic: Ecosystems Structure and Function Lesson: Nitrogen transformations Clarification: Key processes such as fixation, nitrification, assimilation, ammonification, and denitrification linking biology to N availability. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  7. Topic: Ecosystems Structure and Function Lesson: Phosphorus cycle characteristics Clarification: Sedimentary-dominated cycle with no major atmospheric gaseous phase, often making P limiting on long timescales. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  8. Topic: Ecosystems Structure and Function Lesson: Bottom-up vs top-down control Clarification: Resource-driven limitation of higher trophic levels versus predator-driven regulation of lower trophic levels and cascades. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced
