Population Ecology: Population growth rates (density dependent/independent); meta population ecology (colonization, persistence, extinction, patches, sources, sinks); age-structured populations


Core Concepts

  1. Topic: Population Ecology Lesson: Population growth rate Clarification: Change in population size per unit time, typically summarized via per-capita growth rate and finite/intrinsic growth parameters used in exponential and logistic models.   2. Topic: Population Ecology Lesson: Density-independent regulation Clarification: Growth and survival shaped by factors whose effects do not depend on population density (e.g., weather extremes, disturbances), often producing additive mortality.   3. Topic: Population Ecology Lesson: Density-dependent regulation Clarification: Growth and survival shaped by factors whose effects intensify with density (e.g., competition, disease), creating negative feedback that can stabilize populations. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  4. Topic: Population Ecology Lesson: Metapopulation Clarification: A set of spatially separated local populations in habitat patches connected by dispersal, where regional persistence depends on colonization-extinction dynamics. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  5. Topic: Population Ecology Lesson: Colonization and extinction dynamics Clarification: Patch occupancy changes driven by dispersal-mediated colonization and local extinction; core to Levins-type metapopulation models. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  6. Topic: Population Ecology Lesson: Patch structure Clarification: Spatial arrangement and quality/size of habitat patches that determine local population viability and connectivity. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  7. Topic: Population Ecology Lesson: Source-sink dynamics Clarification: Sources have positive local growth and export dispersers; sinks have negative local growth and persist via immigration. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced


  8. Topic: Population Ecology Lesson: Age-structured populations Clarification: Population models that track individuals by age classes, allowing age-specific survival and fecundity to determine growth and stable age distribution. Show: Study Notes | MCQ Basic | MCQ Intermediate | MCQ Advanced
