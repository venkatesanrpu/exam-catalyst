Q1. A radioactive isotope undergoes a decay sequence: α-decay followed by β⁻-decay. If the original isotope is \( ^{226}_{88}Ra \), what will be the final product after both decays?  
A. \( ^{222}_{86}Rn \)  
B. \( ^{222}_{87}Fr \)  
C. \( ^{222}_{85}At \)  
D. \( ^{222}_{88}Ra \)  
**Answer: B**  

**Explanation:**  
- α-decay reduces mass number by 4 and atomic number by 2: \( ^{226}_{88}Ra \to ^{222}_{86}Rn \).  
- β⁻-decay increases atomic number by 1 (neutron → proton): \( ^{222}_{86}Rn \to ^{222}_{87}Fr \).  
- Correct option is B.  
- A: Only after α-decay, not final product.  
- C: Atomic number change incorrect for β⁻ decay.  
- D: No decay applied, wrong.

Q2. In a pure α-emitter with half-life \( t_{1/2} = 1.0\times10^9 \) s, the decay constant λ and fraction of nuclei decayed after 3×10⁹ s are respectively:  
A. λ = 6.93×10⁻¹⁰ s⁻¹, fraction ≈ 87.5%  
B. λ = 6.93×10⁻¹⁰ s⁻¹, fraction ≈ 50%  
C. λ = 3.33×10⁻¹⁰ s⁻¹, fraction ≈ 75%  
D. λ = 6.93×10⁻¹² s⁻¹, fraction ≈ 99%  
**Answer: A**  

**Explanation:**  
- λ = ln(2)/\( t_{1/2} \) = 0.693 / (1×10⁹) ≈ 6.93×10⁻¹⁰ s⁻¹.  
- Fraction decayed after time t: \( f = 1 - e^{-λt} \). For t=3×10⁹ s: exponent = −(6.93×10⁻¹⁰ × 3×10⁹) ≈ −2.079 ⇒ f ≈ 1 − e^{-2.079} ≈ 0.875 (87.5%).  
- B: Fraction wrongly taken as one half-life value only; here time is triple half-life equivalent in terms of exponent magnitude but not integer multiples exactly—must use exponential law, not linear reasoning.  
- C/D: Wrong λ values lead to wrong fractions.

Q3. A nucleus emits a β⁺ particle followed immediately by γ radiation without changing its mass number between these steps. Which statement best describes the process?  
A. β⁺ emission increases neutron count and lowers proton count; γ emission changes both mass and atomic number slightly due to recoil energy loss  
B. β⁺ emission converts a proton to neutron; γ emission releases excess nuclear energy without changing nucleon numbers; atomic number decreases by one only during β-decay step  
C. β⁺ emission converts neutron to proton; γ emission changes mass number but not atomic number; atomic number increases by one during γ step  
D. Both emissions change mass number but not atomic number significantly  
**Answer: B**  

**Explanation:**  
- β⁺ decay: p → n + e⁺ + ν_e ⇒ Z decreases by 1, N increases by 1, A constant.
- γ emission: electromagnetic de-excitation of nucleus ⇒ no change in Z or A.
- Correct is B.
- A: γ does not change nucleon numbers; recoil negligible for Z/A changes.
- C: Confuses β types; β⁺ reduces Z, not increases it.
- D: Mass number remains constant in both steps.

Q4. Consider the decay series starting from \( ^{238}_{92}U \) ending at stable \( ^{206}_{82}Pb \). If only α and β decays occur in this chain, how many α-decays and β-decays must occur?  
A. α=8, β=6  
B. α=8, β=4  
C. α=9, β=6  
D. α=7, β=8  
**Answer: A**  

**Explanation:**  
- Each α-decay reduces A by 4 and Z by 2.
From A=238 to final A=206 ⇒ ΔA=32 ⇒ #α = ΔA/4 = 8.
After α-decays: Z from 92 to (92 −16)=76.
Final Z=82 ⇒ need increase of Z by +6 ⇒ each β− increases Z by +1 ⇒ #β− =6.
Correct option is A.
- B/C/D have incorrect counts from miscalculating ΔZ or ΔA.
